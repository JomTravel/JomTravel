Wat Phothivihan

The temple houses a 40-metre statue of a sleeping Buddha, which is considered as the largest and longest in the state since its construction in 1975.

Upon its completion in 1979, it is recognised as one of the two reclining Buddha statues in Malaysia and also the largest in Southeast Asia until being surpassed by the 66-metre statue in Chaukhtatgyi Buddha Temple of Myanmar.

Muzium Wau

Muzium Wau Kelantan is a small kite museum showing off the types of elaborate kites which are popular in Kelantan and elsewhere in Malaysia.

The museum is located close to Pantai Sri Tujuh and it is worth combining a visit to the museum with a trip to this popular beach.

If you are lucky you may see a demonstration by a skilled craftsman of how kites are made. Also on display are kites from other countries such as Japan, Thailand and Cambodia.

Wat Mai Suwankiri

Located 10 kilometres from Kota Bharu, this temple is famous for its unique dragon boat structure--60 metres long with two curling dragons on each end.

The temple features an eclectic collection of Thai-influenced mural paintings on the ceilings and boasts a 10-metre-tall statue of a standing buddha.

An elegantly decorated hall is under construction near the dragon boat. The temple is renowned for its daily bathing ceremony. Participants believe it cleanses evil and encourages a change of luck.
